We have an explicit formula for the sum of the first \(n\) terms of a geometric series.

Let \(c_k = ar^{k-1}.\) Then we have a sequence of finite sums that can be defined from \(c_k.\)

\[s_n = \sum_{i=1}^{n}c_k = c_1+c_2 +\dots+c_n\]

We have an explicit formula defining \(s_n.\)

\[s_n = \sum_{i=1}^nar^{k-1} = \frac{a\left(1-r^n\right)}{1-r}.\] This formula works as long as \(r \ne 1.\)

\begin{example} \label{finitesumgeoseries}
    Find \(s_7\) for the geometric series with initial term \(a = \sqrt{3}\) and common ratio \(r=\frac{1}{2}.\)
    \begin{solution}
        \[s_7 = \sqrt{3} + \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}+\dots +\frac{\sqrt{3}}{64} = \frac{\sqrt{3}\left(1-1/64\right)}{1-1/2}.\]
        Simplifying we get \(s_7 = \sqrt{3}\frac{63}{32}.\)
    \end{solution}
\end{example}

If \(-1< r < 1\) then we have a formula for the sum over the \emph{entire sequence}.

\[\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}ar^{k-1} = \frac{a}{1-r}.\]

\begin{example}
    Find the sum of the geometric series in \autoref{finitesumgeoseries}.
    \begin{solution}
        \begin{gather*}\sum_{k=1}^{\infty}\sqrt{3}\frac{1}{2}^{k-1} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\frac{1}{2}} = 2\sqrt{3}. \end{gather*}
    \end{solution}
\end{example}
