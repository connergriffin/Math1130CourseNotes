\begin{example}
    You are considering investing \(\$2500\) in one of two accounts. One account offers \(4.5\%\) compounding continuously and another offers \(4.75\%\) compounding quarterly. Which account will provide a greater return?
    \begin{solution}
        We will determine this by comparing effective rates.
        
        Effective rate for \(4.5\%\) compounding continuously: \(e^{0.045}-1 = 0.04603 = 4.603\%.\)
        
        Effective rate for \(4.75\%\) compounding quarterly: \(\left(1+\frac{0.0475}{4}\right)^4-1 = 0.04835 = 4.835\%.\)

        The account offering \(4.75\%\) compounding quarterly will provide better returns.

    \end{solution}
\end{example}
