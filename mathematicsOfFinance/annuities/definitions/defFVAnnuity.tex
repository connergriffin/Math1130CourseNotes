\begin{definition}
    The future value of an annuity is the sum of the \emph{future values} of all \(n\) payments. Again using the sum of a geometric sequence formula, the formula
    \[FV_a = R  \frac{\left(1+r\right)^n-1}{r}\]
    gives the \emph{future value of an annuity} of \(R\) dollars per payment period for \(n\) periods at the interest rate of \(r\) per period.
\end{definition}

We again define the function \(s\left(n,r\right)=\frac{\left(1+r\right)^n-1}{r}.\)
