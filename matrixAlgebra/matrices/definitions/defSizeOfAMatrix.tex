\begin{definition}
    The \emph{size of a matrix} with \(n\) rows and \(m\) columns is \(n \times m.\) The numbers in a matrix are called the entries. In an arbitrary matrix \(A\) the entry in row \(i\) and column \(j\) is called the \(i,j\)th entry and will typically be represented as \(A_{i,j}.\) In certain circumstances, we will use \(\left[A_{i,j}\right]_{n\times m}\) to represent an arbitrary \(n\times m\) matrix with \(i,j\) entry \(A_{i,j}.\) Sometimes we will drop the subscript indicating the size.
\end{definition}

\begin{example}
    \(\begin{bmatrix}
        1 \\ -1
    \end{bmatrix}\) has size \(2 \times 1\) and the \(2,1\) entry is \(-1.\)

    \(\begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 3 \\
        -5 & 7 \\
        2 & 1
    \end{bmatrix}\) has size \(3 \times 2.\) The \(1,2\) entry is \(3\), the \(2,1\) entry is \(-5\) and the \(2,2\) entry is \(7.\)
\end{example}
