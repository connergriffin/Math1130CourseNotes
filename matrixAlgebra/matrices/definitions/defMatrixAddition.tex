We add matrices entry-wise. They have to be the same size.

\begin{definition}
    Suppose \(A\) and \(B\) are matrices of the same size. The sum of \(A\) and \(B,\) denoted \(A+B\) is defined such that the \(i,j\)th entry is

    \[\left( A+B \right)_{i,j} = A_{i,j} + b_{i,j}.\]
\end{definition}

\begin{example}
    \[\begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 3 \\
        7 & 5
    \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 1\\
        1 & 0
    \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
        1+1 & 1+3 \\
        7+1 & 5+0
    \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
        2 & 4 \\
        8 & 5
    \end{bmatrix}\]
\end{example}

PROPERTIES OF MATRIX ADDITION

Assuming \(A,B,C\) have the same size. Then we have the following properties
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(A+B = B+A \)  (commutativity)
    \item \(A + \left( B+C \right) = \left(A+B\right)+C\) (associativity)
    \item \(\left(A+B\right)^T = A^T+B^T.\)
\end{enumerate}
