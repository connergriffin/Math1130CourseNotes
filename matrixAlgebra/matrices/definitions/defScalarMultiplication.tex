\begin{definition}
    A real number is called a \emph{scalar} when dealing with matrices which have real number entries.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}
    Let \(k\) be a scalar and \(A\) a matrix. Then \(kA\) is the matrix with \(i,j\) entry \(kA_{i,j}.\) That is each entry of \(A\) is multiplied by \(k.\)
\end{definition}

\begin{example}
    \[\frac{1}{2}\begin{bmatrix}
        1 & 2 & 5\\
        0 & 1 & 3 \\
        0 & 0 & 1
    \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}
        1/2 & 1 & 5/2\\
        0 & 1/2 & 3/2 \\
        0 & 0 & 1/2
    \end{bmatrix}\]
\end{example}

PROPERTIES OF SCALAR MULTIPLICATION

Let \(A,B\) be matrice with the same size and \(k,\ell\) be scalars. Then we have the following properties.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \(k\left(A+B\right) = kA+kB\) (distributive property for matrix addition)
    \item \(\left(k+\ell\right)A = kA+\ell A\) (distributive property for scalar addition)
    \item \(k\left(\ell A\right) = \left(k \ell\right)A\)
    \item \(\left(kA\right)^T = kA^{T}\)
\end{enumerate}
