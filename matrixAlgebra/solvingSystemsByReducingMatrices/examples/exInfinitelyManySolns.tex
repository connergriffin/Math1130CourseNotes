
\begin{example}
    Find all solutions to the system of linear equations.
    \[\begin{cases}
        x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3 + 3x_4 = 5\\
        x_2 + x_3+2x_4 = 1 \\
        x_1+5x_3+x_4=3
    \end{cases}\]
    \begin{solution}

        This system as an augmented matrix.

        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 3 & \vline & 5 \\
            0 & 1 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 1 \\
            1 & 0 & 5 & 1 & \vline & 3
        \end{bmatrix}\]
        We will let \(R_1, R_2,\) and \(R_3\) be the first, second, and third rows respectively.

        \begin{gather*}\overset{R_3-R_1}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 3 & \vline & 5 \\
            0 & 1 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & -2 & 4 & -2 & \vline & -2
        \end{bmatrix}\\
        \overset{R_3+2R_2}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 3 & \vline & 5 \\
            0 & 1 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 6 & 2 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\\
        \overset{R_3/6}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 3 & \vline & 5 \\
            0 & 1 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 1 & 1/3 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\\
        \overset{R_2-R_3}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 3 & \vline & 5 \\
            0 & 1 & 0 & 5/3 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 1 & 1/3 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\\
        \overset{R_1-R_3}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 0 & 8/3 & \vline & 5 \\
            0 & 1 & 0 & 5/3 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 1 & 1/3 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\\
        \overset{R_1-2R_2}{\longrightarrow} \begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 0 & 0 & -2/3 & \vline & 3 \\
            0 & 1 & 0 & 5/3 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 1 & 1/3 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}
        \end{gather*}
        We may now convert this augmented matrix into a system of linear equations.

        \[\begin{cases}
            x_1 - 2x_4/3 = 3 \\
            x_2 + 5x_4/3 = 1 \\
            x_3 + x_4/3 = 0
        \end{cases}\]

        Note that each of \(x_1, x_2, x_3\) can be expressed as a function in the variable \(x_4.\) This makes \(x_4\) a \emph{free variable.} For any choice we make of \(x_4,\) we will get a unique solution associated with that choice. We can solve for each of \(x_1,x_2,x_3\) and then \(x_4=x_4.\) We can then express all solutions to the system of equations as a matrix.

        \[\begin{cases}
            x_1 = 2x_4/3 + 3 \\
            x_2 = - 5x_4/3 + 1 \\
            x_3 = - x_4/3 \\
            x_4 = x_4
        \end{cases}\]

        We first make a column matrix of the coefficents for \(x_4.\)
        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            2 \\
            -5/3 \\
            -1/3 \\
            1
        \end{bmatrix}\]
        and another column matrix of the constant terms in each equation.
        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            3 \\
            1 \\
            0 \\
            0
        \end{bmatrix}.\]
        Thus every solution to the system of equations is represented by
        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            2 \\
            -5/3 \\
            -1/3 \\
            1
        \end{bmatrix}x_4 + \begin{bmatrix}
            3 \\
            1 \\
            0 \\
            0
        \end{bmatrix}\]
        This is a one parameter family of solutions. The \(x_4\) is the one parameter.
    \end{solution}
\end{example}

There can be more than one free variable. The free variables in a solution to the system of equations correspond to the columns with no leading entry. In the example above, the fourth column -- which is associated with \(x_4\) -- has no leading entry and hence \(x_4\) was the free variable.

\begin{example}
    Find all solution to the system of linear equations.
    \[\begin{cases}
        x_1 + 2x_2 + x_3 + 2x_4 = 3 \\
        x_1 + 2x_2 - x_3 + 2x_4 = 1 \\
        2x_1 + 4x_2 - x_3 + 4x_4 = 3 \\
        x_1 + 2x_2 - 2x_3 + 2x_4 = 0
    \end{cases}\]
    \begin{solution}
        This system as an augmented matrix.
        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 3 \\
            1 & 2 & -1 & 2 & \vline & -2 \\
            2 & 4 & -1 & 4 & \vline & 3 \\
            1 & 2 & -2 & 2 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\]

        We first apply the row operations to zero out all of the entries in the first column other than the leading entry of row one.

        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 3 \\
            0 & 0 & -2 & 0 & \vline & -2 \\
            0 & 0 & -3 & 0 & \vline & -3 \\
            0 & 0 & -3 & 0 & \vline & -3
        \end{bmatrix}\]

        The leading entry of the second row is \(-2.\) We divide the row by \(-2\) and add \(3R_2\) to each of \(R_3\) and \(R_4.\)

        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 1 & 2 & \vline & 3 \\
            0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \vline & 0 \\
            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\]

        The reduced matrix.

        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            1 & 2 & 0 & 2 & \vline & 2 \\
            0 & 0 & 1 & 0 & \vline & 1 \\
            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \vline & 0 \\
            0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \vline & 0
        \end{bmatrix}\]

        Since the second and fourth columns have no leading entry, \(x_2\) and \(x_4\) will be free variables. We have a two parameter family of solutions.

        As a system of equations.

        \[\begin{cases}
            x_1 + 2x_2 +2x_4 =2 \\
            x_2 =x_2 \\
            x_3 = 1 \\
            x_4 = x_4
        \end{cases}\]
        We construct coefficient matrices for each of the free variables and matrix for the constants.

        Every solution to this system of equations can be expressed by this matrix.

        \[\begin{bmatrix}
            -2 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ 0
        \end{bmatrix}x_2 + \begin{bmatrix}
            -2 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 1
        \end{bmatrix}x_4 +\begin{bmatrix}
            2 \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 0
        \end{bmatrix}\]
    \end{solution}
\end{example}
