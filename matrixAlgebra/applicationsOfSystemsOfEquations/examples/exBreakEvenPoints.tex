\begin{example}
A product is sold for \(\$ 12\) per unit. The fixed cost is \(\$2500\) and the variable cost per unit is \(\$ 7.\) Find output and revenue at the break even point and the profit when \(5000\) units are sold.

\begin{solution}
    The revenue function: \(R(q) = 12q\)

    The cost function: \(C(q) = 7q+2500.\)

    The system of equations modeling total revenue and total cost:
    \(\begin{cases}
        s = 12q \\
        s = 7q+2500
    \end{cases}.\)

    This is a linear system of equations so there will be one solution, infinitely many solutions, or no solution.

    By applying either substitution or elimination we find that the break even point is \((500, 6000.)\) The output is \(500\) and the total revenue is \(6000.\)

    Total profit when \(5000\) units are sold will be \(R(5000) - C(5000) = 22,500.\)
\end{solution}
\end{example}
