\begin{question}
    Find the vertex and \(y\)-intercept of the function \(f(x) = \frac{1}{2}x^2+3x-2.\)
    \begin{solution}
        The first step is to label \(a,\) \(b,\) and \(c.\)
        \begin{gather}
            a=\frac{1}{2} \\
            b = 3 \\
            c=-2
        \end{gather}
        The \(y\)-intercept is simply \((0,-2).\) The \(x\)-coordinate of the vertex is \[\frac{-b}{2a} = \frac{-3}{2(\frac{1}{2})} = -3.\] The \(y\)-coordinate is \[\frac{-b^2+4ac}{4a} = f(-3) = \frac{9}{2}-9-2 = \frac{-13}{2}.\]
        The vertex is \(\left(-3,\frac{-13}{2}\right).\)
    \end{solution}
\end{question}
